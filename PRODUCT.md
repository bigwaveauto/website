# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

EV buyers and luxury vehicle buyers researching pre-owned cars online. They've done research before arriving — they know what a Rivian R1T or Cadillac LYRIQ is worth. They want confirmation, not education. They're buying from a distance (nationwide delivery) and need to trust the dealer before they commit. Context: desktop or mobile, mid-funnel, comparing multiple options.

## Product Purpose

Big Wave Auto is a pre-owned EV and dependable luxury vehicle dealership in Sussex, WI. The website is the primary sales surface — no foot traffic required. Success means a buyer submitting an inquiry, reserving a vehicle, or applying for financing. The VDP is the highest-value conversion page on the site.

## Brand Personality

Honest · Effortless · Premium

The brand is what a great independent dealer feels like: direct about price, confident about inventory, zero games. Not flashy. Not corporate. Not a startup trying to be cute. The experience should feel like buying from a trusted expert who respects your time.

## Anti-references

- **CarMax / AutoTrader** — generic, corporate, orange-heavy, filter-heavy, zero personality
- **Carvana** — playful to the point of gimmick, feels like it's hiding behind a mascot
- **Local dealer sites** — cluttered, dated, banner-ad aesthetic, untrustworthy visuals
- The pattern to avoid: any interface that looks like it was built from a dealer CMS template

## Design Principles

1. **Show the number, own the number.** Price, monthly payment, and out-the-door cost are front and center. Never buried.
2. **Confidence, not persuasion.** No urgency tricks, no "limited time" pressure. The car speaks for itself.
3. **Effortless forward motion.** Every scroll should answer the next question in the buyer's head, in order.
4. **Premium through restraint.** Quality is communicated by what's removed, not what's added.
5. **Trust is built, not claimed.** Carfax, window sticker, and real photos are the proof — not badges that say "trusted dealer."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support required (buyers include older demographics). Color contrast must pass 4.5:1 for all body text — no muted-gray-on-gray.
